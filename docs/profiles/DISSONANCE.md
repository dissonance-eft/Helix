### V.B PRIMARY MUSICAL ARCHITECTURE

Across nearly all preferred music, three primary attractors recur.

---

#### 1. Groove Engine (Primary Attractor)

Strongest attractor:

> fused bass + percussion as one continuity-bearing locomotion unit

Bass and percussion are not processed as separate roles.

They are experienced as:
- the moving floor
- the structural spine
- the continuity carrier
- the propulsion body of the track

This engine must do more than keep time.

It must:
- create momentum
- maintain coherence
- allow internal motion
- support repeated listening without collapse into boredom

This is the strongest and most stable musical preference.

Examples of the engine-dominant branch:
- bass music / dubstep / dub-influenced material
- Tool
- Silkie
- Ganja White Night
- groove-heavy jazz
- groove-centered VGM
- certain hip-hop production
- trance only when the engine remains alive rather than formulaic

---

#### 2. Floating Upper Motion (Secondary Attractor)

Second strongest attractor:

> elevated or suspended layers above the groove engine

This includes:
- flute
- woodwinds
- synth haze
- breathy or hovering vocals
- suspended melody
- atmospheric guitar
- expressive upper-register timbres

The function of this layer is not ornament.

It provides:
- lift
- spectral motion
- emotional color
- threshold feeling
- perceptual verticality

Ideal configuration:

> grounded engine below  
> suspended motion above

This is one of the clearest recurring structures across:
- James Blake
- Mitsuda/Uematsu/Shimomura
- Dark Cloud
- FFIX
- Chrono Cross
- Monica Martin
- Aaliyah
- Tennis
- woodwind-heavy soundtrack material

---

#### 3. Coherent Variation (Identity with Internal Freedom)

Third primary attractor:

> stable identity with internal mutation

This appears at multiple scales.

At the track level:
- a groove remains stable while arrangement mutates
- repetition contains meaningful change
- sections evolve without losing the system

At the artist/composer level:
- fingerprint is recognizable
- range is real
- style is not static

This is why strong attraction occurs toward:
- MGMT
- Kendrick Lamar
- Mitsuda
- Uematsu
- Shimomura
- Sakimoto
- Sakuraba
- Meguro
- Tool
- Vampire Weekend

The preference is not for novelty alone.

It is for:

> authored identity that remains intact while changing state

### V.C SECONDARY ATTRACTOR CLUSTERS

The following are not the main engine of taste,
but they recur strongly enough to define major branches.

---

#### Cluster 1 — Crooked Coherence

Definition:

> systems that remain coherent while leaning sideways

This includes:
- uncanny but stable structures
- Halloween-like tonal worlds
- psychedelic but legible motion
- slight rhythmic or harmonic wrongness
- playful hauntedness
- temporal crookedness
- bright systems with one shadow too many

This is not horror.
This is not pure dissonance.
This is not chaos.

It is:

> intact structure under subtle distortion

Strong anchors:
- MGMT, especially *Congratulations*
- early James Blake
- Sega Genesis FM timbres
- Decap Attack-like synthetic eeriness
- Alexandra Savior
- certain VGM threshold tracks

---

#### Cluster 2 — Threshold Attraction

Definition:

> attraction to coherent structures at the edge of legibility

This includes music that feels:
- half-emerged
- half-dissolved
- phasing in and out
- hovering near a boundary
- almost crossing into another state
- present, but not fully accessible

This is broader than transformation or dissolution.

It includes:
- emergence
- disappearance
- return
- hovering
- partial illegibility

The key attractor is:

> knowing the boundary is there, while not fully accessing the other side

This cluster is highly diagnostic of the overall system.

---

#### Cluster 3 — Loop-Seam / Circular Collapse

Definition:

> tension accumulation at a return boundary, followed by collapse back into origin

This is especially salient in VGM.

Preferred pattern:
- a system builds toward a seam
- one or more bars intensify near the end
- layers accumulate
- threshold is reached
- instead of resolving forward, the track re-couples to the beginning

This is not terminal release.

It is:

> circular collapse

This explains strong attraction to:
- pre-loop bars
- seam-build sections
- short return moments that hit harder than entire songs
- VGM composed around modular re-entry

This is one of the clearest structural preferences in the profile.

---

#### Cluster 4 — Coherent Abrasion

Definition:

> attraction to grain, pressure, and strain without structural loss

This includes:
- dark vocal harmonies
- distortion with control
- heavy guitar pressure
- rough edges that remain musical
- harmonic darkness with physical texture

This is why:
- Alice in Chains
- Soundgarden
- grunge-adjacent material
- blues-heavy Zeppelin
- abrasive Tool passages

continue to map cleanly despite sounding unlike softer branches.

The system does not reject abrasion.

It rejects:

> abrasion without form

---

#### Cluster 5 — Elegant Sweetness / Just Sad Enough

Definition:

> beauty softened by restraint, melancholy, or wistful tension

This branch includes:
- graceful melody
- suspended sweetness
- low-drama sadness
- emotional poise
- soft vocal contour
- clear structure with subdued ache

This is why artists like:
- Tennis
- Alexandra Savior
- certain Monica Martin material
- some Blonde Redhead / Beach House adjacent tracks

remain highly attractive despite lower aggression and less overt crookedness.

The preference is not for sentimentality.

It is for:

> sweetness under control

#### Cluster 6 — Bright Authored Pop

This branch is not an exception.

It appears when music provides:
- clarity

- witty / symbolic lyrics

- internal motion beneath a cleaner surface

Key examples:
- Vampire Weekend

This branch proves that the system does not require darkness.

It requires:

> living structure

#### Cluster 7 — Rhythmic-Harmonic Engine Jazz

> jazz-adjacent music where bass and piano function as a unified movement system

Not generic jazz preference.

Specific preference for:
- melodic, authored jazz
- bass as engine rather than background

### V.E LYRIC PROCESSING

Lyrics are usually secondary.

They matter most under one of four conditions:

1. Symbolic / Archetypal  
   → Tool, MGMT

2. Witty / Structurally clever  
   → Kendrick, Vampire Weekend

3. Oblique / impressionistic  
   → MGMT, Vampire Weekend, selected art-pop

4. Inseparable from delivery  
   → Monica Martin, Aaliyah, James Blake, certain pop exceptions

Direct diaristic self-report usually has low pull unless compensated by:
- strong groove
- strong timbre
- strong phrasing
- strong melodic inevitability

This is why some pop or hip-hop exceptions survive despite weak lyrical content.

### V.F LOCAL TRIGGER MODEL

Not all preferred music fits the macro-profile equally.

A secondary mechanism exists:

> local triggers

A song can bypass weak overall alignment if it contains a sufficiently strong micro-event.

Common local triggers:
- anticipatory vocal entry
- phrase finishing early or late against the loop
- syncopated vocal rhythm
- chorus-level inevitability
- semantic blur + timbral clarity
- sudden layer accumulation
- short pre-loop seam builds
- miniature crooked-coherence events
- one section of extreme payoff density

This explains why some isolated pop or outlier tracks remain liked despite weak global alignment.

The system is therefore:

> macro-structured, micro-permissive

- orchestral writing without fingerprint
- generic hip-hop production without textural intelligence

> intelligent motion inside a stable frame
