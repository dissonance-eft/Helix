============================================================
HELIX DISCOVERY ROADMAP
============================================================

Document Type: Research Roadmap
Location: atlas/roadmap/HELIX_ROADMAP.md
Purpose: define the long-term discovery program of Helix.

This document records hypotheses, experimental probes,
and cross-domain discovery paths for the Helix system.

Unlike architectural specifications, entries in this
document represent exploratory research directions.

Ideas recorded here are not assumed to be correct.

They exist to guide experiment design.

------------------------------------------------------------
1. HELIX DISCOVERY PHILOSOPHY
------------------------------------------------------------

Helix is designed to discover structural invariants across
complex systems.

A structural invariant is a pattern that appears across
multiple domains despite differences in implementation.

Helix pursues discovery through:

• cross-domain comparison
• deterministic computational experiments
• reproducible measurement pipelines
• Atlas knowledge graph integration

The goal of the roadmap is to guide the discovery process.

This document should evolve as new observations emerge.


------------------------------------------------------------
2. STRUCTURAL DOMAIN HYPOTHESIS
------------------------------------------------------------

Helix currently studies four structural domains.

waves
symbols
agents
equations

These correspond to different forms of structure.

Music substrate primarily analyzes wave systems.

Language substrate analyzes symbolic systems.

Agent and game systems analyze decision systems.

Mathematics substrate analyzes formal rule systems.

A core research question is whether structural invariants
appear across these domains.


------------------------------------------------------------
3. DISCOVERY PROBE FRAMEWORK
------------------------------------------------------------

Each roadmap entry represents a discovery probe.

A probe includes:

hypothesis
domains involved
signals to measure
experiments to perform

If a pattern appears across multiple domains,
it becomes a candidate invariant.

4. CROSS-DOMAIN PROBES
------------------------------------------------------------

------------------------------------------------------------
PROBE 1
Decision Compression Across Domains
------------------------------------------------------------

Hypothesis

Systems approaching commitment points reduce their effective
decision dimensionality.

Domains

agents
language
music
mathematics

Signals

decision entropy
influence concentration
dimension reduction

Experiments

multi-agent strategy simulations
conversation convergence analysis
music transition structure analysis
dynamical system attractor analysis


------------------------------------------------------------
PROBE 2
Symbol Compression Systems
------------------------------------------------------------

Hypothesis

Symbolic systems compress large structural spaces into
efficient symbolic representations.

Domains

language
music notation
mathematical notation

Experiments

compare grammar complexity vs semantic space
analyze motif notation vs musical structure
evaluate equation compression efficiency


------------------------------------------------------------
PROBE 3
Oscillator Synchronization
------------------------------------------------------------

Hypothesis

Coupled oscillatory systems synchronize when coupling
strength exceeds a critical threshold.

Domains

music rhythms
neural oscillations
network oscillator models

Experiments

Kuramoto oscillator simulations
multi-agent timing coordination

5. HARDWARE-LEVEL PROBES
------------------------------------------------------------

Helix also investigates structure at the hardware constraint
level.

Examples include:

soundchip synthesis
limited channel systems
real-time signal generation

These systems provide controlled environments where
constraints are explicit.
