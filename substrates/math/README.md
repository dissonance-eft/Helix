------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

The Mathematics substrate serves as the theoretical research
arm of Helix.

It investigates the formal structure underlying patterns
observed in other substrates.

Historically this includes research on:

Decision Compression Principle (DCP)
Epistemic Irreversibility Principle (EIP)
collapse geometry
network stability
phase transitions in decision systems


------------------------------------------------------------
2. RESEARCH AREAS
------------------------------------------------------------

dynamical systems
network theory
information theory
decision geometry
collective behavior systems


------------------------------------------------------------
3. REPOSITORY STRUCTURE & PIPELINES
------------------------------------------------------------

The Math substrate adheres to the standard 10-stage Helix
pipeline architecture.

substrates/math/

├── README.md
│   Substrate architecture specification
│
├── ingestion/
│   Generation or import of theoretical models
│
├── parsing/
│   Transformation of models into computable spaces
│
├── structural_analysis/
│   Phase transition and stability analysis
│
├── feature_extraction/
│   Identification of critical thresholds
│
├── domain_analysis/
│   Math-specific topological and geometric analysis
│
├── measurement_synthesis/
│   Synthesis of metrics across theoretical models
│
├── embedding_generation/
│   Latent structural representation of models
│
├── pattern_detection/
│   Cross-model invariant detection (e.g. Map8 mappings)
│
├── atlas_integration/
│   Formal registration of invariants into the knowledge base
│
└── interpretation/
    Theoretical formulation and hypothesis generation.
    Contains the original logic pipelines, laws, and foundational
    documents ported from the legacy Atlas structure:
    - map8 permutation artifacts
    - constraint theories (DCP, EIP)
    - fundamental topological proofs


------------------------------------------------------------
4. RELATIONSHIP TO HELIX
------------------------------------------------------------

The Mathematics substrate provides theoretical explanations
for patterns discovered elsewhere.

Other substrates observe phenomena.

Mathematics attempts to formalize them.
